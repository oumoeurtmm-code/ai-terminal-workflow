The 6 FinOps Principles
① Teams Need to Collaborate

Engineering, Finance, and Business work together — cloud cost is everyone's responsibility, not just IT's problem.

② Everyone Takes Ownership

The team that creates the resource owns the cost. Decentralized accountability with centralized enablement.

③ A Centralized Team Drives FinOps

One FinOps team enables everyone else. They are coaches — not cost police. They set standards, not rules.

④ Reports Should Be Accessible & Timely

Real-time or near-real-time data. Last month's bill is too late. Everyone should be able to see their spend.

⑤ Decisions Driven by Business Value

Cost is not the only metric. Value per dollar matters. Sometimes spending more is the right answer if it drives more value.

⑥ Take Advantage of Variable Cost Model

Cloud's variable cost model is a feature, not a bug. Scale down when not in use. Pay only for what you need.


🎯
Core mindset: FinOps is about maximizing business value from cloud — not minimizing cost. The right answer is never just "spend less." It's "spend smarter."

Key Terms — Rapid Reference
Unit Economics
Cloud cost mapped to a unit of business value (cost per user, per order, per API call)
Showback
Report costs to teams — informational only, no financial consequence
Chargeback
Teams are actually billed for their cloud costs — real financial accountability
Blended Rate
Average cost across on-demand + reserved + spot pricing tiers
Unblended Rate
Actual rate paid for each specific resource at time of use
Amortized Cost
RI/SP upfront costs spread evenly over the commitment term
RI Coverage
% of eligible on-demand hours covered by reserved instances (target ≥70%)
RI Utilization
% of reserved capacity actually being used (target ≥90%)
Forecast Variance
Difference between predicted and actual spend — high variance = poor visibility
Cost Allocation Tags
Labels on resources that must be activated in billing to appear in reports
Anomaly Detection
Automated alerts when spend deviates from expected historical baseline
Cloud CoE
Cloud Center of Excellence — where FinOps team typically sits, sets standards
Lift & Shift
Move workloads to cloud as-is with no re-architecture (may not be cost-optimal)
GreenOps
Cloud sustainability and carbon reduction — intersects with FinOps cost efficiency

Exam Strategy — 8 Tips
Collaborate first — always
FinOps is cross-functional. If one answer involves a single team acting alone vs. Engineering + Finance + Business working together — pick collaborate. Always.
Value over cost savings
The FinOps answer is almost never "spend less." It's "spend smarter" or "tie spend to business value." Cost reduction without context is wrong.
Inform before you Optimize
If an org can't see their spend clearly yet, the right next step is Inform — not Optimize. You can't fix what you can't measure.
The RI / Savings Plan trap
Buying more commitments is NOT always right. If utilization is already low, more commitments = more waste. Optimize usage first, then commit to what you actually need.
Read for maturity clues
"What should this org do NEXT?" — look for clues about where they are in Crawl/Walk/Run. Pick the action that advances them to the next stage, not two stages ahead.
Map persona to action
CFO concerned about surprises → showback/forecast. Engineer annoyed by limits → education + ownership model. Persona → Pain → Domain → Action.
Unit Economics is the north star
When asked how to measure FinOps success, think unit economics first — cost per user, cost per transaction — not total spend reduction.
Open book — use it for definitions
The FinOps Foundation glossary at finops.org is your lifeline. If you blank on a term, don't guess — look it up. You have 1.8 min/question — budget your time.
