# FinOps Core Principles: Inform, Optimize, Operate

FinOps is an evolving cloud financial management discipline and cultural practice that enables organizations to get maximum business value by helping engineering, finance, technology, and business teams to collaborate on data-driven spending decisions.

## 1. Inform Phase
The first phase in the FinOps lifecycle is to provide visibility into cloud spend and usage.
- **Allocation:** Ensure all costs are attributed to the correct business unit, team, or project using tagging and hierarchy.
- **Reporting:** Real-time dashboards and reports to understand current spend.
- **Forecasting:** Predicting future spend based on historical data and business growth.
- **Unit Economics:** Understanding the cost per unit of value (e.g., cost per transaction, cost per customer).

## 2. Optimize Phase
Once visibility is established, the focus shifts to optimizing resources and rates.
- **Rightsizing:** Ensuring instances and services are correctly sized for the workload (avoiding over-provisioning).
- **Rate Optimization:** Utilizing commitments like Reserved Instances (RIs), Savings Plans, and Committed Use Discounts (CUDs).
- **Waste Elimination:** Identifying and removing "zombie" resources (unused volumes, idle load balancers, orphaned IPs).
- **Spot Instances:** Using low-cost, interruptible capacity for non-critical or fault-tolerant workloads.

## 3. Operate Phase
The final phase is to institutionalize these practices into the organization's DNA.
- **Continuous Improvement:** Regularly reviewing metrics and refining processes.
- **Governance:** Implementing policies and automated guardrails to prevent cost overruns (e.g., Cloud Custodian).
- **Alignment:** Aligning cloud spending with business goals and KPIs.
- **Culture:** Fostering a culture where engineers take ownership of their cloud costs.
